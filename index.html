Band overview

A quick reference to major areas of radio you can expand into full pages later.

Longwave Very low frequencies with long-distance ground wave behavior and specialist interest.
Medium Wave / AM Broadcast Band Traditional AM broadcasting, especially interesting for nighttime DX listening.
Shortwave / HF International broadcasting, utility stations, amateur radio, and ionospheric propagation.
VHF / FM Broadcast Local and regional reception, higher audio quality, and mostly line-of-sight coverage.
Amateur Bands Licensed radio activity across multiple frequency ranges and communication modes.

Propagation and reception

One of the coolest parts of radio is that reception is shaped by time, distance, atmosphere, and frequency.

Ground Wave Important for lower frequencies, especially longwave and medium wave over land and sea.
Skywave Key to shortwave and HF, where signals reflect or refract via the ionosphere.
Line of Sight Common with VHF and FM, where coverage is often more local and terrain matters.
Interference & Noise Electrical noise, atmospheric noise, and nearby electronics can all affect reception.

Glossary preview

A glossary page will make your site feel so much more complete. Here’s a strong preview section for the homepage.

DX

Long-distance radio reception or communication, especially when receiving distant stations.

SSB

Single Sideband, a more bandwidth-efficient voice mode that includes USB and LSB.

QRM

Interference from other stations or signals that makes reception more difficult.

QSB

Signal fading over time, common in HF and shortwave propagation conditions.
